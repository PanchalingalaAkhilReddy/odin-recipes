Biryani

Description

Biryani is a fragrant and flavorful rice dish layered with marinated meat, aromatic spices, and herbs. Originating from the Indian subcontinent, it's a celebration of texture and taste.

This Hyderabadi-style chicken biryani is slow-cooked to perfection, combining tender chicken, basmati rice, and a rich blend of spices for a truly indulgent meal.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups basmati rice
  • 500g chicken (bone-in preferred)
  • 1 cup yogurt
  • 2 onions (thinly sliced)
  • 2 tomatoes (chopped)
  • Fresh coriander and mint leaves
  • 2 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
  • Whole spices (bay leaf, cloves, cinnamon, cardamom)
  • 1 tsp turmeric
  • 1 tsp red chili powder
  • Salt to taste
  • Oil or ghee

Steps

  1. Wash and soak basmati rice for 30 minutes.
  2. Marinate chicken with yogurt, spices, and ginger-garlic paste for at least 1 hour.
  3. Fry onions until golden brown and set aside half for layering.
  4. Cook marinated chicken with tomatoes and half the fried onions until tender.
  5. Boil rice until 70% cooked, then drain.
  6. Layer rice over chicken in a heavy-bottomed pot. Add mint, coriander, and remaining onions.

  8. Gently mix and serve hot with raita or salad.
